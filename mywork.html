In my Into to HTML class, we "recreated" a business's website practicing responsive design. Craftsmen Industries was the business I decided on. My father has been employed here for years and complains about the lack of usability their website offers. When reviewing the site myself I noticed they have an extreme number of pages, when many could be combined and shortend. In these examples it shows how I redesigned the Contact page, giving it a more user friendly touch while simplifying the information as well. In this project I used NotePad++.

For my final Project in Into to HTML I made up a bussines and created a website for it . The purpose was to incorporate everything we had learned that semester. Above is more responsive design using the 12 GRID LAYOUT. It is just a practice page from my Into to HTML class. However, it shows the first time I learned that margins ARE NOT the only, and definetly not the easiest, way to align columns! It was great practice but I believe we all will agree that the 12 grid layout is absolutly more user friendly on our end.
